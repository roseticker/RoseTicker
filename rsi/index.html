03 · Financials

The numbers that matter

Toggle between last year and this year. Notice how profit grows faster than revenue — that's called operating leverage.

×3.3
The operating leverage story
Revenue grew 41%. Profit grew 134%. That means profit grew 3.3× faster than sales. Translation: RSI's costs are mostly fixed, so as more players sign up, almost every new dollar drops straight to the bottom line. This is the #1 reason analysts are excited.

Why this matters for RSI

The bad

RSI trades at 76–86× earnings. When interest rates rise, future profits become "less valuable today" (a finance concept called discounting). That's why the stock can fall even when business is great.

The good

Online gambling is surprisingly recession-resilient. When people can't afford vacations or concerts, $20 of slots from the couch is the cheaper thrill. Historical data backs this up.

09 · Risks

What could go wrong

Severity bars below. Tap any risk to expand.

Regulatory volatility High

Online gambling lives or dies at the state legislature. Maryland and Virginia have bills stall for years over union opposition, retail-casino lobbying, and moral concerns. If iGaming legalization slows nationally, RSI's total addressable market gets capped.

Tax-rate increases High

States hungry for revenue keep raising gambling tax rates. Pennsylvania already taxes slot revenue at 54%. New York's proposal is 30.5%. Higher taxes directly compress those juicy net margins.

Macro / consumer pullback Medium

Gaming is resilient but not bulletproof. A deep recession or escalation of the Iran conflict could finally crack consumer spending, especially on entertainment.

Cannibalization debate Medium

Brick-and-mortar casinos argue online gaming will eat their lunch. This argument continues to stall legislation in populous states even though research suggests the products are complementary.

Technical debt / cloud migration Medium

The company itself flagged "strained resources" and a "skills gap" during cloud migration. A major outage, ransomware attack, or DDoS hit could damage the "trust edge" BetRivers has built.

Valuation re-rating High

At 76–86× earnings, the stock prices in years of perfect execution. Any quarter that disappoints could trigger a 20–40% drop, even if the business is fundamentally fine. This is the #1 short-term risk.

12 · Jargon decoder

Translate finance-speak

All those acronyms, in plain English.

EBITDA
"Earnings before the boring stuff." Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization. A rough proxy for cash the business is generating from its core operations.
P/E ratio
Price-to-earnings. How many years of current profit you're paying for one share. RSI's 76× means the market thinks profits will grow a lot.
Operating leverage
When revenue grows faster than costs, so profit grows much faster than revenue. RSI grew profit 3.3× faster than revenue last quarter — that's strong operating leverage.
MAU / ARPMAU
Monthly Active Users / Average Revenue Per Monthly Active User. How many people use the app each month + how much money each one is worth.
GGR
Gross Gaming Revenue. Money wagered minus money paid out to winners. The casino's actual top line before expenses.
CPA
Cost Per Acquisition — how much marketing money it takes to sign up one new paying customer. Lower is better.
LTV
Lifetime Value — total revenue a customer will generate before they churn. A great business has LTV ≫ CPA.
iGaming / iCasino
"Internet gaming." Online slots, blackjack, roulette, etc. Distinct from online sports betting.
Sports book "hold"
The percentage of total bets the sportsbook keeps. Volatile — a bad NFL Sunday can wreck a month.
Discount rate
How much less a future dollar is worth today. When interest rates rise, future profits get discounted more, so high-growth stocks fall.
Recession-resilient
An industry that holds up when the economy weakens. Gaming has historically qualified — cheap entertainment beats no entertainment.
Buyback
Company uses its cash to repurchase its own shares. Reduces share count → each remaining share owns more of the company.
Prediction market
A marketplace where people trade "yes/no" contracts on future events. Polymarket and Kalshi are pressuring traditional sportsbooks.

The $1.5 Trillion Defense Request — Who Benefits

The Pentagon's FY2027 request is the largest ever — roughly $1.5 trillion, about 40%+ above FY2026. Here's where the money concentrates, and the public companies most exposed to each piece, with headline primes plus the suppliers that are often less priced-in.

Status — proposed, not law. This is the President's request plus the House Defense subcommittee markup (~June 10–11, 2026). It still has to clear full committee, both floors, conference, and a signature — figures will change. Notably, ~$350B (including most of Golden Dome) rides on a separate reconciliation bill, not the regular appropriations bill.

🚢 Shipbuilding

$65.8B to build the fleet — and widen the supplier base

What it funds: ~18 battle-force ships + 16 other vessels, toward a 123-ship push, plus an explicit effort to spread work beyond the traditional two yards to add competition and capacity.

Why it matters: shipbuilding is multi-year, sticky revenue. The "widen the base" angle is the interesting part — it could pull dollars to newer or smaller yards and shipyard-tech suppliers, not just the incumbents.

Headline names
HIIHuntington Ingalls — largest U.S. military shipbuilder (carriers, subs)
GDGeneral Dynamics — Electric Boat (submarines), Bath Iron Works
Suppliers / picks-and-shovels
BWXTBWX Technologies — naval nuclear reactors for subs & carriers
LDOSLeidos — uncrewed surface vessels & autonomy
DRSLeonardo DRS — shipboard power & electronics
Private (not investable)
privAustal USA, Fincantieri Marinette Marine — potential beneficiaries of broader competition
🚀 Missiles & Munitions

Multiyear buys + low-cost "mass" and hypersonics

What it funds: multiyear munitions procurement, ~$355M Family of Affordable Mass Missiles (Air Force), and low-cost hypersonic strike (~$325M Army / ~$156M Navy).

Why it matters: the shift toward cheap, mass-produced missiles favors not just the big interceptor makers but the upstart "attritable" players and the rocket-motor supply chain everyone depends on.

Headline names
RTXRTX (Raytheon) — interceptors, effectors, munitions
LMTLockheed Martin — missiles & hypersonics
Suppliers / picks-and-shovels
KTOSKratos — low-cost missiles, targets & hypersonic vehicles
LHXL3Harris — Aerojet Rocketdyne: solid rocket motors (the bottleneck)
NOCNorthrop Grumman — the other major solid rocket motor source
🛰️ Golden Dome Missile Defense

~$17.5B for a homeland missile shield

What it funds: a layered missile-defense architecture — space-based sensors and interceptors, command-and-control. Note: nearly all of it sits in the reconciliation request, with only ~$400M in the base appropriations bill.

Why it matters: the splashiest, most stock-moving line item — a brand-new multi-year program spanning interceptors, space sensors, and the software to tie it together. But the reconciliation dependency makes the timing and totals less certain.

Headline names
LMTLockheed Martin — interceptors, systems integration
RTXRTX — radars & interceptors
NOCNorthrop Grumman — sensors, space, battle management
Suppliers / picks-and-shovels
LHXL3Harris — missile-tracking satellites (HBTSS-type sensors)
PLTRPalantir — command-and-control / data fabric
BAHBooz Allen / Leidos (LDOS) — systems engineering & integration
Private (not investable)
privSpaceX (Starshield), Anduril — widely expected space-layer & software contributors
🛩️ Drones & Autonomy

Scaling low-cost, attritable systems into production

What it funds: moving cheap drones and autonomous "attritable" weapons from prototypes into volume production — a core theme of the request.

Why it matters: this is where newer names can win share against the primes. The catch: several leaders are private, so listed exposure is narrower.

Headline names
KTOSKratos — tactical drones & jet targets
AVAVAeroVironment — small drones & loitering munitions
Private (not investable)
privAnduril, Shield AI — autonomy leaders; only indirect exposure today
💻 Defense Software & Cyber

The connective layer across every program

What it funds: DoD cyber, battle-management software, and the data systems that tie sensors to shooters across all the above.

Why it matters: software scales better than hardware and rides every program at once — a pick-and-shovels play on the whole budget rather than one weapon.

Headline names
PLTRPalantir — targeting, C2, data integration
Suppliers / picks-and-shovels
LDOSLeidos, CACI, SAIC — defense IT & integration services
☢️ Nuclear Forces

Modernizing the triad

What it funds: the land-based ICBM replacement (Sentinel), plus warhead and naval-reactor work supporting the deterrent.

Why it matters: decades-long programs with very few qualified suppliers — durable, defensive revenue regardless of near-term budget fights.

Headline names
NOCNorthrop Grumman — Sentinel ICBM prime
Suppliers / picks-and-shovels
BWXTBWX Technologies — naval reactors & nuclear components
LHXL3Harris — solid rocket motors for the missile

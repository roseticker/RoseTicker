02 · What is SMH?

An ETF, explained simply

An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is a basket of stocks you can buy with one ticker. Buy one share of SMH and you instantly own 26 semiconductor companies in one trade.

Simple version
Think of it like a smoothie. Instead of buying 26 separate fruits (chip stocks), you buy one cup that has all of them blended in the right ratios. The "recipe" is set by an index — VanEck just follows it.

What kind of ETF is this?

  • Thematic — focused on one industry (chips), not the whole market
  • Market-cap weighted — bigger companies get more weight
  • Passive — tracks the MVIS US Listed Semiconductor 25 Index
  • Liquid — trades ~10M+ shares daily, easy to buy/sell

Why this matters for SMH

The bull case

AI is causing a structural shift in compute spending. Every major company is building data centers. Every data center needs chips. SMH owns 26 of the companies that supply them. Even mature analog chip makers like Texas Instruments (4.4% of SMH) have raised prices 4 times in 2 years — the entire industry has pricing power.

The bear case

Chip cycles always end. When the AI capex wave peaks — whether 2027, 2028, or later — semis fall hard. Historically, the industry has had 40–60% drawdowns every 3–5 years. Plus, valuations at 51× P/E already price in years of perfect growth.

06 · Why semiconductors?

The structural case for chips

⚡

The "picks and shovels" play

Gold rush analogy: in the 1849 Gold Rush, most prospectors lost money — but the people selling picks and shovels got rich. SMH lets you own the picks-and-shovels of the AI boom without betting which AI company wins.

🏭

Insanely high barriers to entry

A modern chip fab costs $20–30 billion to build. ASML (3.9% of SMH) makes the only machines on Earth that can print 2nm chips — and they cost $300M each. New competitors can't just show up.

🌍

Government tailwind

The US CHIPS Act, EU Chips Act, and similar programs in Japan/Korea/India are funneling hundreds of billions in subsidies to onshore chip production. SMH's US-listed holdings benefit directly.

09 · Risks

What could go wrong

Severity bars below. Tap any risk to expand.

Single-stock concentration (NVDA) High

NVIDIA is ~16% of the fund. If NVDA reports a bad quarter or competition (like d-Matrix or custom hyperscaler chips) erodes its 90%+ AI GPU market share, SMH gets hit much harder than a broad market ETF would.

Taiwan / TSMC geopolitical risk High

TSMC is ~10% of SMH and manufactures the chips for ~90% of the other holdings. Any escalation around the Taiwan Strait — even just news flow — moves the entire fund. China's military activity near Taiwan is the single biggest tail risk for the global chip industry.

Cyclical industry / capex bust High

Semis are boom-bust. Every 3–5 years there's a 40–60% drawdown when hyperscaler capex pauses or inventory corrects. The current AI cycle has lasted unusually long — when it pauses, SMH falls hard.

Valuation / multiple compression High

P/E of 51× on a basket of cyclical names is rich. Even if earnings keep growing, if the market re-rates semis to a "normal" 25–30× P/E, SMH falls 40%+ without a single company missing estimates.

New AI accelerator competition Medium

Hyperscalers (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Meta MTIA) and startups (d-Matrix, Cerebras, Groq) are building custom chips that bypass NVIDIA. If they win even 20% market share, SMH's top holding takes a serious hit.

Export controls / China decoupling Medium

US export restrictions to China keep tightening. China was a major customer for NVDA, AMD, and equipment makers (LRCX, KLAC, AMAT, ASML). Each new restriction trims revenue from the basket.

Currency & ADR risk Low

TSMC and ASML are foreign companies trading as ADRs. Big moves in the USD/TWD or USD/EUR show up in SMH performance. Smaller risk but worth knowing.

12 · Jargon decoder

Translate ETF-speak

ETF
Exchange-Traded Fund. A basket of stocks (or bonds) you can buy as a single ticker. Trades like a stock all day, but you own a slice of everything inside.
AUM
Assets Under Management. Total money the fund holds. Bigger AUM = more liquid, less risk of fund closure.
Expense ratio
Annual fee charged by the fund manager, expressed as % of AUM. 0.35% means $35/year on a $10K investment. Lower is better.
Market-cap weighted
Bigger companies get more weight in the fund. NVDA is 16% of SMH because NVDA is the biggest company in the index, not because it's the "best."
P/E ratio
Price-to-earnings. How many years of profit you're paying for one share. SMH's 51× means the basket is priced for years of strong growth.
Beta
How much a stock/ETF moves vs. the broader market. SMH's beta of 1.34 means it moves about 34% more than the S&P in both directions.
HBM
High Bandwidth Memory. The specialty memory that AI accelerators (like NVIDIA GPUs) need. Currently the biggest supply bottleneck in AI hardware.
Hyperscaler
The biggest cloud companies: Microsoft (Azure), Google (Cloud), Amazon (AWS), Meta. Their capex spending is the single biggest demand driver for SMH's holdings.
Capex
Capital expenditure — money companies spend on big physical investments (data centers, fabs, equipment). Surging capex = more chip orders.
Foundry
A chip manufacturing plant. TSMC is the largest foundry in the world. Most semi companies design chips but don't make them — they pay TSMC to.
Fab
Short for "fabrication plant." Same as foundry. Costs $20–30 billion to build a modern one.
Node
The size of transistors on a chip. Smaller is more advanced (2nm beats 3nm beats 5nm). Cutting-edge nodes are how chips keep getting faster.
ADR
American Depositary Receipt. How foreign companies (like TSMC, ASML) trade on US exchanges. Subject to currency effects.
Rebalance / reconstitution
When the index updates its holdings or weights. SMH rebalances quarterly. Forces the fund to sell winners and buy laggards — automatically.
Tracking error
How closely the ETF follows its underlying index. Lower is better. SMH's is very small.
CHIPS Act
US legislation providing ~$52B in subsidies for domestic chip manufacturing. Several SMH holdings have received grants.

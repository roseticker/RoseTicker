What the company does

Vistra both generates electricity (~38,700 MW across nuclear, gas, coal, solar and batteries) and sells it directly to ~4–5 million homes and businesses (brands like TXU Energy). Owning both ends is a built-in hedge: when wholesale power is expensive its plants earn more; when it's cheap its retail arm buys cheaper.

Vistra Vision — the "growth engine"

The zero-carbon side: the #2 competitive U.S. nuclear fleet (6,400+ MW), plus solar and battery storage ("Vistra Zero"), plus the cash-generative retail business. Nuclear runs 24/7 and is carbon-free — exactly what AI data centers want.

Why nuclear is the prize: it's the scarce, always-on, carbon-free power that data centers are racing to lock up — giving Vistra a "scarcity premium."

Vistra Tradition — the "reliability core"

The efficient natural-gas fleet (plus remaining coal) that can ramp up on demand. As renewables grow, this dispatchable power is the backstop that keeps the grid stable during peak demand or low wind/sun.

Plain terms: nuclear is the steady base; gas is the flexible muscle that fills the gaps. Together they sell into the fast-growing Texas (ERCOT) and Mid-Atlantic (PJM) markets.

The AI / data-center growth story

This is the core reason VST has re-rated. AI data centers need enormous amounts of 24/7 carbon-free power, and Vistra has signed long-term deals to supply it — turning power plants into contracted, predictable revenue.

Meta · 20-yr deal · 2,600+ MW nuclear (PJM) Amazon (AWS) · 20-yr · up to 1,200 MW (Comanche Peak) NVIDIA / KKR venture · "Helix" · preferred power provider

Why these deals matter

20-year contracts lock in revenue for decades, which funds nuclear plant life-extensions and "uprates" (squeezing more megawatts from existing reactors — 433 MW added, the largest corporate-backed uprate program in the U.S.). The NVIDIA-backed Helix Digital Infrastructure venture (with KKR and Kuwait's wealth fund) names Vistra the preferred power supplier for a $10B data-center build-out.

Buying more capacity

Vistra is also expanding its flexible gas fleet to meet surging demand:

  • Lotus (closed Nov 2025)~2,600 MW of modern gas plants across the Midwest, Northeast and California.
  • Cogentrix (announced Jan 2026, ~$4.0B)10 gas facilities, 5,500 MW; expected to close mid-to-late 2026 and add to cash flow from 2027.
  • Nuclear license extensionsPerry approved to 2046; Comanche Peak filed to run to 2050/2053 — long runway.
  • PJM capacity "windfalls"Tight grid supply has pushed capacity prices to caps, boosting wholesale earnings in 2026–27.

Bull case, bear case & key risks

👍 Bull case

  • "AI power" scarcity premiumOwns scarce 24/7 carbon-free nuclear that data centers urgently need.
  • Locked-in long-term revenue20-year deals with Meta & Amazon, plus the NVIDIA-backed Helix venture.
  • Profitable & investment-grade$5.9B EBITDA, ~$3.6B free cash flow, BBB− ratings.
  • Shareholder-friendly~30% of shares bought back since 2021; 6 years of dividend growth.
  • Rising capacity pricesTight PJM grid is pushing prices up, lifting wholesale earnings.

👎 Bear case

  • A lot of optimism in the priceThe stock has re-rated on the AI theme; high expectations leave less margin for error.
  • Earns on volatile power pricesA drop in power/commodity prices would hit EBITDA and guidance.
  • Regulatory uncertaintyFERC rules on data-center "co-location," and PJM/ERCOT market redesigns, could change the economics.
  • Big-ticket M&A & ratesDeals like Cogentrix ($4B) and projects are sensitive to high interest rates.
  • Nuclear operating riskAn outage or safety issue at a major plant would directly dent earnings.

Risks, ranked

HIGH — Power-price & commodity exposure. Guidance relies on healthy realized power prices; a market downturn flows straight to earnings.

MEDIUM — Regulatory/policy. Pending FERC decisions on data-center interconnection and possible cost-shifting could slow the AI-demand tailwind.

MEDIUM — Execution & integration. Closing and integrating Cogentrix, plus new builds, while keeping leverage near 3.0x.

MEDIUM — Interest rates. A capital-intensive business; higher rates raise the cost of acquisitions and growth projects.

LOWER but severe — Nuclear safety/outage. Unlikely, but a major event at a key plant would be material.

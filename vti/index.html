02 · What is VTI?

"Just buy the whole market"

VTI tracks the CRSP US Total Market Index — a fancy name for "basically every US company you've ever heard of, and 3,000 you haven't." When you buy one share, you instantly own a sliver of Apple, Tesla, Coca-Cola, your bank, that biotech in San Diego, and a quirky semiconductor company in Idaho.

The Boglehead Bet
Instead of trying to guess which 10 stocks will do best, you bet on American business as a whole. Some companies in VTI will go to zero. Others will become the next Apple. The ETF automatically up-weights the winners and shrinks the losers. You just keep buying.

How big is "the whole market"?

  • Large-cap (top ~500 companies): 72–92% of the fund
  • Mid-cap (next ~800): 5–21% of the fund
  • Small-cap (~2,200 companies): 1–8% of the fund
  • Total: ~3,700 stocks. About 100% of the investable US market.

04 · The Magnificent Seven

You already own all seven

When you buy VTI, you don't need to also "buy NVIDIA." You already own it — and Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, Broadcom, and Meta. Together with Tesla they form the "Magnificent Seven," the seven mega-caps that make up about 28% of the entire fund.

The VTI vs VOO debate, settled
Over the last 10 years, VOO has edged VTI (15.6% vs 15.1% annualized) because large-caps dominated. But in 2026, small-caps trade at a 16× P/E vs the S&P's 27× — the biggest valuation gap in 20 years. If smaller companies have their decade, VTI pulls ahead. Picking VTI is essentially a free option on a small-cap comeback.

08 · What could go wrong

The honest risk list

"Just buy the index" sounds bulletproof. It isn't. Here's what to watch for.

Tech & Magnificent-7 concentration High

36% of VTI is technology, and the top 7 stocks alone are ~28% of the fund. If AI sentiment cracks or regulators force breakups of Apple/Google/Meta, VTI takes a hit much bigger than its name suggests. "Total market" is more concentrated than ever.

Valuation — priced for perfection High

VTI trades at a 27× P/E — well above its 30-year average of ~18×. After +30% last year, a lot of optimism is already baked in. A simple "multiple compression" back to 22× would knock 15–20% off the fund without a single earnings miss.

Persistent inflation / higher-for-longer rates Medium

The Fed is holding rates near 3.5% to fight sticky inflation. Higher rates mean higher discount rates, which mathematically reduce the present value of future tech earnings. VTI's tech-heavy mix is the part of the market most sensitive to this.

"It IS the market" — no outperformance possible Medium

VTI is designed to be the market — so it can never beat the market. If you're looking for alpha (excess return) you won't find it here. You're getting beta (market return), efficiently. Whether that's a risk depends on your goals.

Behavioral — selling at the bottom High

The biggest risk to your VTI returns isn't VTI — it's you. The average investor underperforms the market by 2–3% per year mostly because they panic-sell during drawdowns. If you can't stomach watching $100K become $65K without flinching, VTI's design assumption breaks.

Home-country bias (no international exposure) Medium

VTI is 100% US. If the next decade belongs to emerging markets or Europe, VTI misses it. Many advisors recommend pairing VTI with VXUS (Vanguard Total International) to get true global exposure. The classic 70/30 US/intl split is a defensible default.

No ESG screening Low–Personal

VTI owns everything — including tobacco, defense, oil & gas, and companies in active controversies. If avoiding "sin" stocks matters to you, you'll need an ESG-tilted alternative.

11 · Translate ETF-speak

Jargon, plain

ETF
Exchange-Traded Fund. A basket of stocks that trades like a single stock. VTI is one ETF that holds ~3,700 companies.
Expense Ratio
The annual fee, expressed as a percentage. VTI's 0.03% means you pay $3 per year per $10,000 invested. Mathematically negligible.
Market-cap weighted
Bigger companies get a bigger slice. Apple ($4T) gets 20× more of your money than a $200B company.
CRSP Total Market Index
The benchmark VTI follows. Includes virtually every US-listed stock above ~$15M market cap.
Banded reconstitution
Instead of bumping a stock between "small" and "mid" cap the second it crosses a line, the index uses a buffer zone. This reduces unnecessary trading and saves you money in taxes.
Turnover
How much the fund buys/sells per year. VTI's 2.6% is incredibly low — most active funds churn 50–100%, racking up tax bills you pay.
Tracking Error
How far the fund drifts from its index. VTI's lifetime tracking error is -0.04% — essentially perfect replication.
Beta
How much the fund moves relative to the broad market. VTI's beta is 1.01 — it moves almost exactly with the S&P 500.
Sharpe Ratio
Return per unit of risk. VTI's 0.87 means it has historically delivered solid returns for the volatility it asks you to stomach.
Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
Buying the same dollar amount on a fixed schedule, regardless of price. Buys more shares when cheap, fewer when expensive. The default Boglehead move.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline before recovery. VTI's worst was -55% in 2008. It got back to its previous high in 2013.
Magnificent Seven
Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, NVIDIA, Meta, plus Tesla (the original 7) or Broadcom (which has overtaken Tesla in market cap). Either way, the mega-cap tech stocks that have driven most US market returns since 2020.

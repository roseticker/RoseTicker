H.R. 1 · 119th Congress

One Big Beautiful Bill Act — Who Stands to Benefit

The law spends and taxes by sector, not by company. Below, its actual provisions (with section numbers) are mapped to the publicly traded companies most exposed to each — grouped by theme. Company names are illustrative sector leaders, not endorsements, and are not named in the bill itself.

How to read this: "What the bill does" cites real sections of the Act. "Who benefits" lists representative public companies an analyst would expect to gain from that provision — this is interpretation, not a list from the statute. See the bottom for clean-energy names the bill works against.
⚔️ Defense & Aerospace

Military build-up & weapons procurement

Title II / Title VII (defense funding): new money for shipbuilding (§20002), integrated air & missile defense (§20003), munitions & defense supply chain (§20004), scaling low-cost weapons/drones into production (§20005), air superiority (§20007), nuclear forces (§20008), and DoD cyber (§20006).

Tens of billions in additional procurement flows to prime contractors and their suppliers — especially missiles, shipbuilding, munitions, drones, and the nuclear deterrent.

🛢️ Oil, Gas & Coal

Expanded fossil-fuel leasing & lower royalties

Title V (Energy & Natural Resources): reinstated/mandated onshore & offshore oil-and-gas lease sales, reduced onshore royalty rates (§402x), Arctic access, plus coal leasing (§50201) and reduced coal royalties (§50202).

More federal acreage, faster permitting, and lower government take improve the economics of domestic drillers, coal miners, and the pipelines that move the output.

🛂 Border Security & Immigration Enforcement

Wall, detention & enforcement build-out

Titles IX & X (Homeland Security / Judiciary): funding for border infrastructure & wall system (§90001) and ~$45B for single-adult detention capacity (§90003), plus expanded surveillance and removal operations.

Large appropriations for detention beds, construction, surveillance tech, and enforcement data systems flow to private-detention operators and government-services/tech contractors.

GEOGEO Group — private detention; direct beneficiary of $45B detention funding

PLTRPalantir — ICE/DHS case & targeting data systems

FLRFluor / Sterling Infrastructure (STRL) — wall & border construction
🏭 Domestic Manufacturing & Capital Investment

Full expensing & factory write-offs

Title VII (Finance / tax): 100% bonus depreciation & higher §179 expensing (§70306), full expensing of U.S. R&D (§70302), and a special depreciation allowance for "qualified production property" — i.e., new factory buildings (§70307).

Capital-heavy manufacturers, chipmakers building U.S. fabs, and equipment makers get to immediately deduct big plant and R&D outlays — boosting after-tax cash flow and incentivizing domestic capex.

INTCIntel / Micron (MU) — new U.S. chip fabs qualify for factory expensing
CATCaterpillar / Deere (DE) — equipment demand rises with capex incentives
GEVGE Vernova / Eaton (ETN) — factory & grid build-out beneficiaries
multiCapital-intensive U.S. manufacturers broadly — faster write-offs improve cash flow
📡 Telecom & Wireless

Spectrum auctions reopened

Title IV (Commerce): reauthorizes and mandates FCC spectrum auctions (§40002), freeing up airwaves for commercial wireless use.

Carriers gain access to new mid-band spectrum to expand 5G capacity; tower and equipment suppliers benefit from the build-out that follows.

TMUST-Mobile US — aggressive spectrum buyer
VZVerizon / AT&T (T) — spectrum for 5G capacity
AMTAmerican Tower / Crown Castle (CCI) — towers used in build-out
🚧 Infrastructure & Materials

Construction tied to wall, shipyards & the Arctic

Titles II, IV, VI & IX: shipbuilding industrial-base spending (incl. turbine generators), Coast Guard Arctic Security Cutters / icebreakers, highway-related funds, and border construction.

Heavy construction, aggregates, and industrial-equipment firms supply the materials and machinery for these federally funded builds.

VMCVulcan Materials / Martin Marietta (MLM) — aggregates for construction
STRLSterling Infrastructure / Fluor (FLR) — large federal construction
CATCaterpillar — construction equipment
🏢 Real Estate & Asset Management

Opportunity Zones & "Trump accounts"

Title VII (Finance): permanent renewal & enhancement of Opportunity Zones (§70421) and new tax-advantaged "Trump accounts" child-savings pilot (§70204).

Permanent OZ tax breaks support real-estate developers and homebuilders in designated areas; new child investment accounts channel long-term flows to asset managers and brokerages.

BLKBlackRock — asset manager for new account flows
SCHWCharles Schwab / fund complexes — custody & investing of "Trump accounts"
homebuildHomebuilders & OZ developers — Opportunity-Zone real-estate incentives
⚠️ On the other side — clean-energy headwinds

Companies the bill works against

Title VII, Subtitle (§70501–70513): terminates the consumer & commercial clean-vehicle credits, residential clean-energy credit, clean-hydrogen credit, and restricts/terminates the clean-electricity production & investment credits.

Removing these subsidies is a direct headwind for EV makers, residential & third-party solar, and hydrogen — the mirror image of the fossil-fuel boost above. Included for balance, not as beneficiaries.

TSLATesla / Rivian (RIVN) — loss of EV purchase credits
RUNSunrun / Enphase (ENPH) — residential solar credit terminated
FSLRFirst Solar — clean-electricity credit restrictions (mixed)
PLUGPlug Power — clean-hydrogen credit terminated

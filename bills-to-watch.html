⚔️ FY2027 Defense AppropriationsCommittee

Historic ~$1.5T request: ships, missiles & Golden Dome

Status: House Defense subcommittee markup (~June 10–11, 2026). Key money: ~$65.8B shipbuilding (18+ ships, push to widen beyond two yards); low-cost "mass" missiles & hypersonics; ~$17.5B Golden Dome missile defense (mostly via reconciliation).

Why it matters: a 40%+ jump over FY2026 floods procurement into missiles, shipbuilding and missile defense — multi-year contracts that show up as backlog.

🏥 FY2027 Labor-HHS AppropriationsCommittee

NIH research funding & health programs

Status: House bill released June 4, 2026 and moving through committee. Key money: NIH research budget, public-health and hospital-related programs. The NIH topline is the swing factor for the life-sciences ecosystem.

Why it matters: federal research dollars flow first to the "tools" companies and contract labs that every funded experiment buys from — often steadier than betting on a single drug.

🪙 Crypto Market Structure & StablecoinsLawCommittee

GENIUS Act (implementing) + CLARITY Act (advancing)

Status: GENIUS Act (stablecoins) is law — regulators must finalize rules by July 18, 2026. CLARITY Act (digital-asset market structure) advanced Senate Banking 15–9 on May 14, 2026; note it bars interest/yield on idle stablecoin balances. Analysts give crypto market-structure ~50–60% odds of passing in 2026.

Why it matters: clear rules let banks, brokers and payment firms move into crypto custody and stablecoins — turning a gray area into a licensed business line.

Headline names
COINCoinbase — largest U.S. exchange; benefits from legal clarity
HOODRobinhood — retail crypto trading
Second-tier / picks-and-shovels

MAMastercard / Visa (V) — stablecoin payment rails
CRCLCircle — USDC stablecoin issuer (directly shaped by the rules)
